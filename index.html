Life in

The Ocean

The Great White Shark
Great White Sharks are the largest predatory fish and can be found in cool
waters close to the coast. They can grow up to 4.6 meters long and can swim over
60km per hour! The Great White can have a set of 300 sharp teeth to help chow
down it's prey. Their menu generally consists of sea mammals such as sea lions,
seals and baby whales. New born Great Whites are called pups. These incredible
creatures are at the top of the food chain!
The Humpback Whale
Humpback Whales get their name from the hump they have in front of their small
dorsal fin. These whales can grow from 15 to 19 meters long and can weigh
around 40 tons! Humpback Whales are baleen whales which means that they
have filter feeding systems in their mouths. Their food mostly consists of krill,
anchovies, sardines and mackerel. Humpback Whales can live up to 50 years
but some have observed them to live longer.
The Killer Whale
The Orca, also know as the Killer Whale, is the largest member of the dolphine
family. They are known for their long dorsal fin and their black and white coloring.
The black and white coloring helps camouflage them by obscuring their outline in
the water. Orcas are apex predators and can swim around 56km per hour. They
feed on different types of prey such as fish, seals, sea birds and squid. Orcas are
social creatures and they live in family groups called pods!
